# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mixed audience: recruiters and hiring managers who scan at speed, and senior engineers or tech leads who read the detail. Both groups view this on desktop at work, evaluating whether Hoh Kok Young is worth a conversation. The scanner needs a confident first impression in under 30 seconds; the reader needs signal density that rewards the extra time.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio and resume site for Hoh Kok Young, Software Engineer based in Singapore. Demonstrates range across full-stack, AI/ML, enterprise infrastructure, and engineering leadership while conveying the judgment and craft behind the work. Success looks like a visitor closing the tab wanting to reach out.

## Brand Personality

Considered, Crafted, Quiet. Confident without loudness. Every choice deliberate, nothing decorative for its own sake. Voice is spare and specific: real numbers, real constraints, no buzzwords. Tone is assured, not eager.

## Anti-references

- Generic dev portfolio: dark mode with neon accents, floating card grids, GitHub contribution graphs, "I'm a passionate developer" openers, hero-metric stat widgets
- Sites that try to impress through visual spectacle rather than through the quality of the work itself

## Design Principles

1. **The portfolio reflects the engineer.** Deliberate choices in the design signal deliberate choices in the code. Sloppiness here reads as sloppiness everywhere.
2. **Readable at speed, rewarding at depth.** A 30-second scan should answer "who is this and are they worth calling?" A 5-minute read should leave the person genuinely impressed.
3. **Show the work, not the wrapper.** The content — the projects, the roles, the numbers — is the hero. Design serves it; design does not compete with it.
4. **Restraint as signal.** Quiet confidence beats decoration. What is left out matters as much as what stays.
5. **Specific over generic.** Every line names something real: a system, a metric, a constraint. No adjective that could appear on anyone's resume.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Body text contrast 4.5:1 or better against all backgrounds. Reduced-motion alternative for every animation. Semantic HTML throughout.
